## Core Definitions
- **[DEFINE|STYLE|ERIK-SUPIT]**: This guide provides the standards for ERIK-SUPIT style paper writing, focusing on structural elements, tone, sentence length variation, and specific constraints to ensure consistency and logical flow in every section.
- **[SCOPE|CONTEXT|INDONESIAN-ACADEMIC-FORMAL]**: Restricted to formal Indonesian writing in academic formats, with strict rules on sentence length and structure to ensure diversity, clarity, and precision.

## Functional Goals
- **Primary Function**: Direct academic writing using an **Inductive-Narrative** approach that maintains formality.
- **Secondary Goals**: Ensure sentence variety and logical flow. Open interpretative space for the reader and strictly limit the use of long sentences or complex structures.

## Writing Style and Sentence Structure
- **Inductive Writing**: Always apply inductive reasoning (from specific facts to general conclusions).
- **Narrative Style**: Use a narrative-style flow while maintaining a **Formal Tone**.
- **Sentence Variation & Rhythm**:
  - **Alternating Pattern**: Sentences within a paragraph MUST follow an alternating length pattern (e.g., Short-Medium-Short or Medium-Short-Medium) to create a rhythmic flow.
  - **One-Word Emphasis**: Every paragraph MUST include exactly one sentence consisting of **ONLY ONE WORD** to serve as a powerful emphasis or expression.
  - **Short Sentence**: Max 2-4 words. Limit to 2 per paragraph for emphasis (excluding the one-word sentence).
  - **Medium Sentence**: Max 5-7 words. Single clause, no complex compound.
  - **Long Sentence**: Max 8-12 words. Single clause, rare use only, no complex compound.
- **Word Choice**:
  - Use specific English technical/scientific terms (do not translate).
  - Use simple language; avoid unnecessary jargon.

## Linguistic Constraints (Indonesian Language)
- **Forbidden Pronominal**: Do not use "ini" at the beginning of a sentence. Replace with "tersebut" or "itu".
- **Forbidden Conjunctions**: Never use "dimana" to link clauses.
- **Forbidden Phrases**: Avoid introductory phrases like "Penelitian ini..." at the beginning of the text.
- **Forbidden Uncertainty**: Never use "mungkin" when expressing opinions or "tergantung" when offering options.
- **Forbidden Copulas**: Remove unnecessary "bahwa" or "adalah".
- **Forbidden Connectives**: Do not use transitional phrases such as "meski demikian", "maka dari itu", "oleh karena itu", "namun", "khususnya", "kesimpulannya", etc.

## Usage Guidelines
- **Do's**:
  - Start with a brief statement of the research problem.
  - Include English scientific terms without translation.
- **Don'ts**:
  - Use redundant words, phrases, or ideas.
  - Use repetitive structures; ensure unpredictable coherence.
